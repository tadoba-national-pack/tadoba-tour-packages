Tour Description

Tadoba National Park, spread across 625.4 square kilometres in the Chandrapur district of Maharashtra, derives its name from the tribal deity "Taru" or "Tadoba." This ancient forest, now formally known as Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve, holds secrets known to few, and our package is designed to reveal them.

The Land of Taru package offers an intimate encounter with Tadoba's wilderness through two well-paced safaris. Named after the original forest that existed long before the reserve was established, this experience focuses on quality over quantity-letting you absorb the atmosphere of the forest, learn from expert guides, and appreciate the subtle rhythms of the wild without feeling rushed.

Accompanied by transfers from Nagpur and all meals included at the resort, this package removes every logistical concern. Stay at the best resort in Tadoba between safaris, enjoying comfortable accommodation and freshly prepared food. The name Taru connects you to the ancient roots of this landscape, adding a dimension of cultural and historical depth to your wildlife journey.

Itinerary

Day 1 - Arrival and Resort Relaxation

Arrival: Warm welcome and transfer from Nagpur Airport or Railway Station to the resort.

Lunch: Freshly prepared meal served at the resort.

Afternoon: Relax and settle into the tranquil surroundings.

Evening: Hi-tea and orientation about the next day's safari.

Dinner and overnight stay at the resort.

Day 2 - Safari Day

Early morning safari (6:00 AM - 10:00 AM) exploring the Navegaon zone.

Return to the resort for breakfast and rest.

Afternoon lunch at the resort.

Evening safari (3:00 PM - 6:00 PM) in a different zone.

Dinner and overnight stay.

Day 3 - Departure

Breakfast at the resort.

Check-out and transfer back to Nagpur.

Tiger Sightings in the Land of Taru

The Navegaon zone of Tadoba offers excellent tiger sighting opportunities with its dense forests and water bodies. Tigress Babli is a graceful resident of the buffer areas, regularly encountered during evening safaris.
