Kolara Safari Zone, Tadoba

A diverse safari zone known for rich wildlife and beautiful landscapes

Kolara Zone

About Kolara Safari Gate

Kolara offers one of the most varied terrains in Tadoba, making every safari a unique adventure.

Kolara is one of the most scenic entry points into Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve. Located on the eastern boundary, this zone features rugged terrain with steep hillocks, deep valleys, and mixed forests that create a dramatic backdrop for wildlife safaris. The diverse topography of Kolara supports a wide range of species, making it a favourite among photographers and seasoned wildlife enthusiasts.

The Kolara zone is crisscrossed by seasonal streams and has several water holes that attract animals throughout the year. The landscape transitions from dense bamboo thickets in the lower reaches to open grasslands on the plateaus, providing varied habitats that sustain tigers, leopards, sloth bears, wild dogs, gaurs, and an impressive array of bird species.

This zone is the territory of the powerful male tiger Bajrang, whose regular appearances thrill visitors. Kolara also sees the emerging male Chota D, who is gradually establishing his own territory. The Kolara zone is known for offering more intimate safari experiences due to lower visitor numbers compared to Moharli. Explore Kolara zone safaris through our Tadoba National Park packages.

Zone Highlights

Why Explore the Kolara Zone?

Kolara's unique landscape and wildlife make it an essential destination for any Tadoba visit.

Varied Topography

Kolara's hills, valleys, streams, and meadows create distinct micro-habitats. This diversity supports a wider range of species than any other zone.

Abundant Wildlife

Tigers, leopards, wild dogs, sloth bears, and gaurs are all regulars in Kolara. The zone's healthy prey base ensures consistent predator activity.

Photography Hotspot

The dramatic light through Kolara's hills and the open meadows create stunning photographic conditions. Sunrise and sunset safaris here are exceptional.

Best Season

October to June is the safari season. Kolara is particularly rewarding in winter (November to February) when the vegetation is thinner and visibility is excellent.

Rich Avifauna

Kolara's habitat diversity attracts over 200 bird species. Look for Crested Serpent Eagles, Changeable Hawk-Eagles, Indian Pittas, and racket-tailed drongos.

Peaceful Experience

Kolara receives fewer visitors than Moharli, offering a more tranquil safari experience. Perfect for those seeking solitude with nature.

Famous Tigers

Resident Tigers of Kolara Zone

The Kolara range is home to magnificent tigers that define the wilderness of this zone.

Bajrang

Bajrang is one of the most powerful male tigers in the Kolara zone. His muscular build and confident stride make him a spectacular sight during safaris. Bajrang's territory covers the prime areas of Kolara, and our expert guides track his movements regularly for guests on Tadoba safari packages.

Chota D

Chota D is an emerging male tiger gradually establishing his territory within the Kolara zone. Early morning safaris offer the best chance of encountering him as he patrols his growing domain. His presence is a promising sign of the healthy tiger population in this zone.
